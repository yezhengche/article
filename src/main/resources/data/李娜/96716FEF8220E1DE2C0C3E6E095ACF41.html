教师简介：李娜


日期：2017-04-06   | 来源：   | 作者：


李娜，重庆大学人文社会科学高等研究院特聘研究员、博士生导师，澳大利亚悉尼技术大学公众史学中心研究员（Research Associate），加拿大康科迪亚大学口述历史叙事中心国际研究员 （International Affiliate），美国印第安纳-普渡大学人文学院“国际学者”，及上海师范大学人文与传播学院兼职教授。

李娜致力于公众史学在中国的发展和学科建设。她是第一位在美国接受完整公众史学训练的中国学者，并拥有近十年在亚洲、欧洲和北美洲主要历史城市的实地研究经验。主要研究领域包括以公众史学为主导的城市历史文化保护，涵盖城市记忆、世界历史城市比较、历史保护政策分析及公众参与等。 她的专著Kensington Market: Collective Memory, Public History and Toronto’s Urban Landscape(《集体记忆、公众历史与城市景观：多伦多市肯辛顿街区的世纪变迁》)University of Toronto Press, 2015 （多伦多大学出版社，2015）被誉为“为研究北美主要城市的历史景观作出理论和方法上的重要贡献”。她的研究在国际城市规划界权威的Oxford Handbook of Urban Planning以及公众史学领域的核心学术期刊The Public Historian和Public History Review发表。她还是Public History Weekly的主要撰稿人。

李娜于2006-2012年先后在美国麻塞诸塞大学环境研究所与加拿大康克迪亚大学口述历史与数字叙事中心担任科研工作。2013年春，任重庆大学人文社会科学高等研究院访问学者；同年秋，作为青年百人引进人才受聘于重庆大学。她发起并组织了“全国公众历史会议”（2013年，苏州），中国首届公众史学高校师资培训（2014年，上海）及中国第二届公众史学高校师资培训（2015年，重庆）。

Dr. Na Li (Lina)is a Research Fellow of the Institute for Advanced Studies in Humanities and Social Sciences (IAS) at Chongqing University (China). She holds a Ph.D. in Urban and Regional Planning from the University of Massachusetts Amherst. Inher role as a Research Fellow at Chongqing University and Adjunct Professor at Shanghai Normal University, Lina is at the forefront to research and establish public history in China. She has organized the first National Conference on Public History in China (2013), the first Public History Faculty Training Program in China (Shanghai, 2014), and the second Public History Faculty Training Program in China (Chongqing, 2015).

Her research focuses on integrating public history in urban preservation. Her first book,Kensington Market: Collective Memory, Public History, and Toronto’s Urban Landscape(University of Toronto Press, 2015) incorporates collective memory in urban landscape interpretation, and suggests a culturally sensitive narrative approach (CSNA) to urban preservation. Her articles appeared inThe Public Historian,Public History Review,The Oxford Handbook of Urban Planningand a number of premium Chinese journals. She is also a regular contributor (core author) forPublic History Weekly, a multilingual blog-journal for history and civics education.

Lina contributes to a better understanding of public history at an international scale. She is frequently invited to lecture about public history in China, Canada, Australia, and the United States. She is International Affiliate of the Centre for Oral History and Digital Storytelling at Concordia University (Canada), an Associate of Australian Centre for Public History at University of Technology Sydney (Australia), and International Scholar and Visiting Research Fellow at the Indiana University School of Liberal Arts at Indiana University-Purdue University Indianapolis (IUPUI).

Lina is completing her second bookAn Introduction to Public History(Chinese), to be published by Peking University.
